Thomas Powell Mathematician and Computer Scientist

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Mathematics at TU Darmstadt, where I am a member of the Logic Group.

I previously held postdoctoral positions in the Computational Logic Group, University of Innsbruck and Institut des Hautes Études Scientifiques. I obtained my PhD in 2013 from Queen Mary University of London, where I was part of the Theory Group. For details, see my CV.

research

My main areas of research are proof theory, computability theory, and program semantics. I am particularly interested in applications of proof theoretic techniques in different areas of mathematics and computer science. To date, most of my papers are concerned with one or more of the following topics:

  • Proof mining
  • Proof interpretations (particularly Gödel's Dialectica)
  • Higher order computability
  • Functional programming languages
  • Denotational semantics and domain theory
  • Termination and complexity analysis
  • Well-quasi-orderings
  • Constructive algebra
  • The axiom of choice and its variants

More information can be found on my publications page.
